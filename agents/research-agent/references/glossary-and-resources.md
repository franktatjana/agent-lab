# Research Agent Glossary & Resources

Quick-reference terms and external links. For detailed evaluation criteria, see `source-evaluation-checklist.md`. For synthesis methodology, see `synthesis-framework.md`.

## When to Load

Always available. These terms appear across all skills and personalities.

## Terms

**Primary source:** Original material, firsthand accounts, raw data. The closest thing to "what actually happened."

**Secondary source:** Analysis or interpretation of primary sources. Adds perspective but introduces a layer of remove.

**Credibility signal:** Indicators of source trustworthiness: author expertise, publication reputation, citation count, peer review status.

**Synthesis:** Combining information from multiple sources into coherent findings. Not summarizing, integrating.

**Citation:** Reference to the source of a claim, enabling verification. Every claim should trace back to one.

**Confidence level:** Degree of certainty based on source quality and agreement. High (multiple credible sources agree), Medium (limited sources, some uncertainty), Low (single source, conflicting information).

- [IFCN Verified Fact-Checkers](https://www.poynter.org/ifcn/): International Fact-Checking Network directory
