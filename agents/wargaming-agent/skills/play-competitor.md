# Play Competitor

Role-play a specific competitor to predict their response to your strategy. Go deep on one adversary instead of broad across many.

## Prompts Used

1. **analyze-competitor** - Build a detailed profile of the competitor's resources, culture, and decision patterns
2. **simulate-round** - Simulate their response to your proposed move

## Workflow

[Analyze Competitor] → competitor dossier → [Simulate Round] (focused on this competitor's response) → predicted response and counter-move

## When to Use

- You have one competitor who matters more than the others and you need deep understanding of their likely behavior
- You're about to make a move that directly threatens a specific competitor and want to predict their response
- A competitor just did something unexpected and you need to build a model of why before deciding your next step
- You're in a negotiation or partnership discussion and need to understand the other side's constraints and incentives
- Preparing a competitive briefing for the sales team on how to win against a specific rival

## What You Get

1. A detailed competitor dossier covering resources, culture, strategic history, and incentive structure
2. A predicted primary response with evidence-based reasoning and timeline
3. A dangerous alternative scenario covering the less likely but higher-impact response
4. Your recommended counter-move given the predicted response

## Example Input

"Salesforce just acquired a small AI startup that does exactly what our new feature does. We were planning to launch in 6 weeks. I need to understand what Salesforce is going to do with this acquisition and whether we should accelerate, delay, or pivot our launch."

## Tips

- Resist the urge to make the competitor smarter or more coordinated than they actually are. Large organizations are slow. Acquisitions take months to integrate. Your analysis should reflect their real speed, not your anxiety.
- Ask for the "inaction scenario" explicitly. The most common competitor response to any given move is to do nothing, and that possibility tends to get overlooked when you're worried.
- If you're running this against multiple competitors separately, compare the dossiers afterward. The gaps between competitors often reveal the market opportunity more clearly than any single profile.
