# Identify Schwerpunkt

Find the center of gravity where concentrated effort produces a disproportionate strategic advantage.

## Prompts Used

1. **set-scenario** - Map the competitive landscape and current resource deployment
2. **find-schwerpunkt** - Analyze leverage points and identify the decisive point for concentration of force

## Workflow

[Set Scenario] → strategic landscape → [Find Schwerpunkt] → concentration brief with decisive point, reinforcement targets, and resource reallocation

## When to Use

- Resources are spread across too many initiatives and none of them are winning
- Leadership is debating priorities and needs a framework for deciding where to concentrate
- The strategy has multiple "top priorities" and the organization can't execute all of them well
- You've completed a wargame and need to determine where to focus based on what the simulation revealed
- A new competitive threat requires reallocating resources and you need to know where to pull from and where to push

## What You Get

1. A force map showing where resources are currently deployed and where they're being wasted
2. A leverage analysis identifying the points where small inputs create disproportionate outputs
3. The Schwerpunkt stated in one sentence, the decisive point that changes the competitive situation
4. A concentration recommendation naming what to reinforce, what to starve, and what risks to accept
5. A specific resource shift plan moving capacity from low-leverage to high-leverage positions

## Example Input

"We're running four product lines, three geographic expansions, and two platform partnerships simultaneously. Engineering is at 120% capacity, every team lead says they're understaffed, and none of the initiatives are hitting their milestones. Something has to give but nobody can agree on what to cut. Where should we concentrate?"

## Tips

- The Schwerpunkt should make people uncomfortable. If the recommendation doesn't require stopping something that feels important, you haven't pushed hard enough on concentration.
- This skill works best after a wargame (design-wargame skill), where the simulation results reveal which competitive moves actually produce results vs. which ones just keep you busy.
- Revisit the Schwerpunkt quarterly. The decisive point shifts as competitors move and markets evolve. What was the right concentration 6 months ago might be the wrong one today.
