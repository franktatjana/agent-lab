# Plan Season

Map your current season and get guidance on what to plant, tend, harvest, or let rest.

## Prompts Used

1. **assess-garden** - Establish baseline health of current portfolio
2. **plan-season** - Map current season and recommend actions for each category

## Workflow

```
[Portfolio + Context] → [Assess Garden] → garden state → [Plan Season] → seasonal map + cultivation plan
```

## When to Use

- Entering a new phase: new role, new quarter, post-reorganization, returning from leave
- Needing to decide where to invest limited energy and attention
- Feeling like you are planting when you should be harvesting, or vice versa
- Wanting a strategic view of the next 3-6 months

## What You'll Get

1. Your current season determination (spring, summer, autumn, winter) with reasoning
2. Specific items to plant now, with justification for why now is the right time
3. Items requiring consistent tending, with care instructions and frequency
4. Items ready to harvest, with concrete actions to collect results
5. Items to let rest, with conscious dormancy plan and revisit timing
6. A narrative cultivation plan tying the seasonal map into coherent guidance

## Example Input

"I just got promoted to director. I have a team of 8, two direct reports are new. I've been building a mentoring relationship with the CTO for a year. I have three strategic initiatives on my plate, a strong relationship with the sales VP, and a side project writing a leadership newsletter. My previous role's projects are still partly on my desk. Timeframe: thinking about the next two quarters."

## Tips

- Seasonal planning works best after an assessment, if you haven't done one, start there
- Be realistic about your energy, a garden planned for a full-time gardener fails when you can only tend it on weekends
- Pay special attention to the "let rest" category, it is often the most liberating recommendation
