# Tend Portfolio

Full portfolio review combining garden assessment, seasonal planning, pruning recommendations, and timing guidance into a comprehensive cultivation plan.

## Prompts Used

1. **assess-garden** - Survey portfolio health and growth stages
2. **plan-season** - Map the current season and plan accordingly
3. **recommend-pruning** - Identify what to thin, prune, or let go
4. **diagnose-timing** - Provide timing guidance for items the user is uncertain about

## Workflow

```
[Full Portfolio + Context + Timeframe + Concerns] → [Assess Garden] → health report
  → [Plan Season] → seasonal map
  → [Recommend Pruning] → pruning recommendations
  → [Diagnose Timing] → timing guidance
  → comprehensive cultivation plan
```

## When to Use

- Annual or quarterly portfolio review
- Major life or career transition requiring a full reassessment
- Feeling lost about priorities and wanting a comprehensive view
- First time using the Gardener Agent and wanting the complete picture

## What You'll Get

1. Complete garden assessment with all items categorized
2. Seasonal map with planting, tending, harvesting, and resting guidance
3. Pruning recommendations with emotional difficulty ratings
4. Timing guidance for items you are uncertain about
5. A comprehensive narrative cultivation plan weaving everything together

## Example Input

"I'm a VP of Product at a Series C startup, 2 years in. Here's my full portfolio: running a 25-person product org, mentoring 3 senior PMs, building relationship with new CEO (6 months in role), driving an enterprise pivot strategy, maintaining partnership with a key customer, writing thought leadership on LinkedIn, learning AI/ML fundamentals, serving on a nonprofit board, and trying to develop my public speaking. My concern: I feel like I'm tending everything and cultivating nothing. Timeframe: next 6 months. I know I need to let some things go but I can't figure out which ones."

## Tips

- This is the most comprehensive skill, give it everything you have on your plate
- Include things you feel guilty about, unfinished projects, neglected relationships, abandoned skills
- Be honest about your energy level and constraints, the pruning recommendations depend on it
- This works best as a periodic review, consider running it quarterly
