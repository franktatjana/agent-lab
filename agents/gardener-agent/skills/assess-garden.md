# Assess Garden

Survey your portfolio of initiatives, relationships, and skills to understand what is healthy, what needs attention, and what is competing for resources.

## Prompts Used

1. **assess-garden** - Survey each item, identify growth stage, categorize by health condition

## Workflow

```
[Portfolio + Context + Concerns] → [Assess Garden] → categorized health report
```

## When to Use

- Starting to feel overwhelmed by how many things you are managing
- Haven't taken stock of all your active initiatives in a while
- Suspecting some things are being neglected while others get too much attention
- Wanting a clear picture before making decisions about priorities

## What You'll Get

1. Each portfolio item categorized by growth stage (seed through dormant)
2. Health assessment across five conditions: healthy, needs attention, overwatered, neglected, ready to harvest
3. Resource competition analysis showing where items crowd each other
4. Overall garden health summary identifying the dominant pattern

## Example Input

"I'm a senior product manager, 3 years in role. I'm running a platform migration (18 months in), mentoring two junior PMs, building a relationship with the new VP of Engineering, writing a blog series on product strategy, and learning data science on the side. I also have a half-finished internal tools project that I haven't touched in months. Feeling stretched thin."

## Tips

- Include everything, even the things you feel guilty about neglecting
- Be honest about how much time you are actually spending on each item
- Mention items you have been meaning to start but haven't, those are seeds still in the packet
