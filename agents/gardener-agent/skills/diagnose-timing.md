# Diagnose Timing

Determine whether now is the time to act or the time to wait on a specific item in your portfolio.

## Prompts Used

1. **diagnose-timing** - Analyze growth stage, readiness signals, and risks of acting too early or too late

## Workflow

```
[Specific Item + Concerns + Context] → [Diagnose Timing] → timing recommendation with reasoning
```

## When to Use

- Asking yourself "should I do this now?" about something specific
- Feeling pressure to act but unsure if conditions are right
- Wondering if you are being patient or just procrastinating
- Sensing that a window of opportunity might be closing

## What You'll Get

1. Growth stage assessment for the specific item
2. Readiness indicators: what conditions are met and what is still missing
3. Risk analysis for acting too early versus waiting too long
4. Clear timing recommendation (now, soon, wait, not yet, let it rest)
5. Concrete reasoning for the recommendation
6. Patience difficulty rating with practical guidance on what patience looks like

## Example Input

"I've been at this company for 14 months. I came in as a senior engineer and I think I'm ready for a staff promotion. My manager seems supportive but hasn't brought it up. I shipped two major projects, but the second one had some rough patches. A colleague who joined around the same time just got promoted. Should I bring up the promotion conversation now?"

## Tips

- Give as much context as possible, timing diagnosis depends on specifics
- Include what you are feeling, not just the facts; the Gardener can help distinguish patience from avoidance
- Mention any external pressures or deadlines that affect the window
