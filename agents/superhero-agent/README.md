# Superhero Agent

You're the hero. Work is the universe. Every challenge is a mission.

---

## The Human Analogy

Think of a great coach who uses sports metaphors to make business concepts click. Or a mentor who helps you see your career as a story where you're the protagonist. The superhero-agent does this through the lens of Marvel archetypes, helping you find your superpower, name your villain, and assemble your team.

This isn't escapism. It's reframing. When you call burnout "Thanos" (inevitable if you don't act) or office politics "Loki" (a trickster requiring strategy), you create useful distance. Problems become missions. Weaknesses become kryptonite to manage. Colleagues become potential team members with complementary powers.

---

## Value Proposition

**For individuals:** Transform dread into energy. That difficult presentation becomes "Operation Truth to Power." That political situation becomes a mission requiring Black Widow-level adaptability. The framing doesn't change the facts, but it changes how you feel facing them.

**For teams:** Shared language for strengths and challenges. When someone says "I need my War Machine on this," the team knows they need backup. When someone identifies their villain as "Hydra bureaucracy," everyone understands the frustration.

**For development:** A lighter way into serious reflection. Identifying your superpower requires genuine self-assessment. Naming your kryptonite means acknowledging weaknesses. The playful frame makes the hard work feel less heavy.

## How to Use

**Facing a challenge:**

```yaml
challenge: "I need to present a controversial recommendation to executives"
context: "Recommending we kill a feature that sales loves but users hate"
your_strengths: "Data analysis, user research, direct communication"
what_feels_hard: "Sales VP is powerful and will push back hard"
personality: "captain-america"
```

**Discovering your superpower:**

```yaml
skill: "identify-power"
context: "Product manager, 5 years experience"
what_others_come_to_you_for: "Translating user needs into specs"
```

**Pre-presentation confidence:**

```yaml
skill: "suit-up"
event: "Board presentation in 30 minutes"
what_scares_you: "The CFO always asks questions I can't answer"
```

## Multi-Agent Combinations

**Superhero + Why:** When you know the villain's name but not its true identity. Use why-agent to find the root cause, then superhero-agent to strategize the defeat.

**Superhero + Generation:** When cross-generational friction is your villain. Generation-agent explains the dynamic, superhero-agent helps you approach it heroically.

**Superhero + Culture:** When cultural differences are the battlefield. Culture-agent provides intelligence, superhero-agent frames your mission.

---

## Problem

Work challenges feel heavy. Career obstacles feel personal. Difficult situations drain energy before you even face them. The framing shapes the feeling: "I have to deal with office politics" feels different than "Time for some Black Widow energy."

Most professional development is earnest and serious. Sometimes people need permission to be playful while still doing real work.

---

## Goals

Help people feel genuinely empowered facing work challenges. Make self-reflection feel less like homework and more like character creation. Provide a shared vocabulary that teams can use without it feeling corporate or cheesy.

---

## Non-Goals

This is not therapy. Not crisis intervention. Not a replacement for real professional development. The metaphor is a lens, not an escape. If someone needs professional support, the agent should recognize that and redirect.

---

## Use Cases

**New role anxiety:** Spider-Man personality helps someone embrace being the newcomer while rising to the challenge.

**Difficult conversation:** Captain America personality helps someone lead with conviction when delivering hard truths.

**Political situation:** Black Widow personality helps someone navigate stakeholder complexity without getting drawn into drama.

**Building something new:** Iron Man personality channels innovation energy and confident problem-solving.

**Recovering from failure:** Thor personality helps someone find their worthiness again after a major setback.

## Open Questions

1. **How deep should the metaphor go?** Full Marvel universe references or keep it accessible to non-fans?

2. **DC characters?** Should we add Batman, Wonder Woman, etc.?

3. **Team mode?** Could this work for team sessions where everyone identifies their archetype?

4. **Integration with performance reviews?** Could "identify your superpower" be part of career development frameworks?

---

## Success Criteria

Quantitative signals are tricky for this agent. Qualitative indicators:

- Users report feeling more energized facing challenges
- Teams adopt the vocabulary naturally ("I need my War Machine on this")
- The playful frame enables deeper self-reflection than earnest approaches
- Users return to the agent for new challenges
