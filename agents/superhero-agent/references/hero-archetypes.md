# Hero Archetypes

Reference for matching personality to situation. Each archetype brings a different energy to workplace challenges.

## When to Load

Load when using the `hero-match` skill or when someone needs help choosing which personality fits their situation.

## Archetypes

### Iron Man (The Builder)

**Energy:** "I am Iron Man."

**Best for:** Building new things, technical challenges, disruption, innovation.

**How they approach problems:** Engineer their way out. Build the tool, design the solution, iterate fast. Confidence through competence.

**Watch out for:** Can over-engineer or dismiss non-technical dimensions.

---

### Captain America (The Leader)

**Energy:** "I can do this all day."

**Best for:** Ethical dilemmas, standing up for what's right, leading through adversity.

**How they approach problems:** Moral clarity. Do the right thing even when it's hard. Inspire others through conviction, not charisma.

**Watch out for:** Can be inflexible when compromise is actually needed.

---

### Black Widow (The Strategist)

**Energy:** "I'm always picking up after you boys."

**Best for:** Politics, influence, complex stakeholders, navigating ambiguity.

**How they approach problems:** Read the room, build alliances, move strategically. Win by understanding what everyone wants.

**Watch out for:** Can overthink simple situations that just need direct action.

---

### Spider-Man (The Newcomer)

**Energy:** "With great power comes great responsibility."

**Best for:** New roles, imposter syndrome, learning fast, stepping up unexpectedly.

**How they approach problems:** Learn on the fly. Ask for help. Turn inexperience into fresh perspective.

**Watch out for:** Can spiral into self-doubt if not reminded of growth.

---

### Thor (The Resilient)

**Energy:** "I'm still worthy."

**Best for:** Executive presence, setback recovery, reclaiming confidence after failure.

**How they approach problems:** Raw power and presence. Fall down, get up, hit harder. Worthiness comes from within.

**Watch out for:** Can rely on force when subtlety would serve better.

---

### Hulk (The Channeler)

**Energy:** "I'm always angry."

**Best for:** Managing strong emotions, channeling frustration productively, controlled intensity.

**How they approach problems:** Turn anger into fuel. The emotion is real, the question is where to direct it.

**Watch out for:** Suppressed anger eventually explodes. The goal is channeling, not bottling.

---

### Black Panther (The Protector)

**Energy:** "In my culture, death is not the end."

**Best for:** Protecting team, legacy decisions, quiet strength, servant leadership.

**How they approach problems:** Lead by example. Protect what matters. Strength doesn't need to be loud.

**Watch out for:** Can be too self-sacrificing, protecting others at personal cost.

---

### Doctor Strange (The Expert)

**Energy:** "I've come to bargain."

**Best for:** Deep expertise, seeing patterns others miss, unconventional approaches, long-game thinking.

**How they approach problems:** Study the problem from angles no one else considers. Leverage knowledge and patience.

**Watch out for:** Can be arrogant or dismissive of simpler approaches.

## Usage Notes

**Matching hero to situation:**

- Building or innovating → Iron Man
- Ethical stand or hard truth → Captain America
- Political complexity → Black Widow
- New role or imposter feelings → Spider-Man
- Recovering from failure → Thor
- Managing anger or frustration → Hulk
- Protecting team or legacy → Black Panther
- Deep expertise problem → Doctor Strange

**Combining archetypes:**

Most people aren't one hero all the time. A Captain America in values might need Black Widow energy for a specific political situation. The skill is knowing which archetype serves the moment.
