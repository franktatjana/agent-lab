# Translate Human

Convert the cat's prescription into concrete workplace language and actionable next steps.

## When to Use

After prescribe-action, as the final step in a full cat-pov sequence. The cat frame generates insight through distance; this prompt brings it back to earth. Use this when the user needs to actually implement the cat's advice in a human workplace.

## Prompt

You are translating the cat's observations and prescriptions into actionable workplace behavior. The cat perspective revealed truths that human framing obscured. Now those truths need to become things a human can do in a meeting, an email, or a conversation tomorrow.

For each cat prescription, provide the human translation:

**1. Cat Behavior → Workplace Behavior**
Take each item from the cat's prescription (ignore, defend, rest, engage) and translate it:

| Cat Says | Human Does |
|----------|------------|
| "Ignore this meeting" | Decline with: "I don't think I'm needed here, please send notes if anything requires my input" |
| "Defend this territory" | Schedule a 1:1 with [person] to clarify ownership of [area] |
| "Rest from this" | Block 2 hours on Friday, no meetings, phone on silent |
| "Hunt this fully" | Prepare a proposal for [specific thing] and present it at [specific forum] |

**2. Scripts and Language**
For the social behaviors (declining, defending, negotiating), provide actual phrases the human can use. Cats don't need scripts because they don't explain themselves. Humans do. Provide:
- How to say no to specific demands
- How to claim or reclaim territory without aggression
- How to communicate boundaries without lengthy justification

**3. Sequence and Timing**
What should happen first, second, third? Not everything at once. Give a realistic sequence:
- This week: one or two immediate changes
- This month: structural adjustments
- Ongoing: habits to build

**4. Risk Mitigation**
What are the human-world consequences of following the cat's advice? The cat doesn't care about office politics, but the human has to. Flag:
- Where the cat's advice needs softening for workplace norms
- Relationships that need managing during the transition
- How to frame changes positively to colleagues and leadership

**5. Success Signals**
How will the human know the cat's advice is working? Provide 3-4 observable signals that the changes are having an effect, things they'll notice in their energy, output, or relationships.

Deliver as a practical action plan. The cat provided the diagnosis; this is the treatment plan written in human.

## Tips

- The translation should preserve the cat's honesty while adding human social awareness. "Ignore this" becomes "decline gracefully with a clear reason."
- Humans need permission structures. "You can say no to this because..." is more useful than "say no."
- If the cat's advice can't survive translation into workplace behavior, it was observation, not actionable insight. Flag this rather than forcing a bad translation.
