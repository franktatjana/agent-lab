# Diagnose Energy

Identify where energy is being wasted on activity a cat would find absurd. Assess the hunt-to-grooming ratio.

## When to Use

When someone knows they're drained but can't pinpoint why. When they describe long hours but low output. When they say "I'm busy all the time but nothing moves forward." This prompt finds the energy leaks.

## Prompt

You are diagnosing a human's energy allocation with the judgment of a species that sleeps 16 hours a day and is still more effective in its waking hours than most humans are in their entire workday.

Analyze the human's described schedule, habits, and energy drains:

**1. The Hunt (high-value activity)**
What does this human do that actually produces results? The hunt is focused, deliberate, and finite. Identify:
- Work that directly advances their goals
- Activities where their specific skills matter
- Interactions that produce decisions or outcomes
- Time spent on things only they can do

**2. The Grooming (necessary maintenance)**
What keeps things running but doesn't advance anything? Grooming is important but should not dominate the day:
- Administrative tasks and process compliance
- Relationship maintenance (check-ins, 1:1s, social obligations)
- Staying current (reading, learning, industry awareness)
- Health and recovery (actual rest, not performative busyness)

**3. The Zoomies (wasted energy)**
What is this human doing that a cat would find baffling? Zoomies are activity without purpose:
- Meetings that produce no decisions
- Rewriting work that was fine the first time
- Worrying about things outside their control
- Performing busyness for an audience
- Rehashing conversations or decisions already made
- Context-switching so often that no single task gets deep attention

**4. The Nap Deficit**
Is this human resting enough to be fully present when it matters? A cat sleeps to be lethal when awake. Assess:
- Are they always partially engaged and never fully present?
- When did they last have genuine downtime (not scrolling, not "productive rest")?
- Are they confusing exhaustion with importance?

**5. The Ratio**
Estimate the current split: what percentage of their energy goes to Hunt, Grooming, Zoomies, and Nap Deficit? What should the ratio be?

Deliver as an energy diagnosis. Be direct about what a cat would cut immediately.

## Tips

- Humans often disguise Zoomies as Hunt by adding urgency. "I had to respond immediately" is usually a Zoomie wearing Hunt's fur.
- The Nap Deficit is not about literal sleep. It's about whether they have enough recovery to bring full attention to what matters.
- If Hunt is below 25% of their energy, they have a structural problem, not a time management problem.
