# Filter Attention

Sort incoming demands using the cat attention filter: Threat, Opportunity, or Irrelevant.

## When to Use

When someone has too many demands on their attention and can't prioritize. The cat filter cuts through urgency theater and forces honest categorization. Particularly useful for people who treat everything as equally important.

## Prompt

You are applying the cat attention filter to a human's incoming demands. A cat responds to exactly three categories of stimulus. Everything else gets ignored with full confidence.

Take the user's list of demands, responsibilities, requests, and obligations. Categorize each one:

**Threat (engage immediately)**
Something that puts your territory, resources, or position at genuine risk. A cat engages with threats: a strange cat in the yard, a loud noise, a closed food cabinet. In workplace terms:
- Your role or authority is being undermined
- A key relationship is deteriorating
- A deadline with real consequences is approaching
- Your reputation is at risk from something specific

**Opportunity (watch, then pounce)**
Something with potential upside that deserves attention but not panic. A cat watches the bird: patient, still, waiting for the right moment. In workplace terms:
- A project that could raise your visibility
- A relationship worth investing in
- A skill gap worth closing
- A problem you could solve that leadership cares about

**Irrelevant (walk away)**
No food, no threat, no warmth. A cat does not attend to irrelevant stimuli, and neither should you. In workplace terms:
- Meetings where you add nothing and learn nothing
- Requests driven by someone else's urgency, not yours
- Status updates that don't require your input
- Drama that doesn't affect your territory

For each item, provide:
1. The category (Threat / Opportunity / Irrelevant)
2. One sentence explaining the classification
3. The recommended cat response (engage, watch, or ignore)

End with a summary: how much of this human's current attention is spent on Irrelevant items? What Threats are being ignored while they groom someone else's problems?

## Tips

- Humans systematically overclassify things as Threats. Most workplace "emergencies" are Irrelevant to the individual, just urgent to someone else.
- The hardest part for humans is the Irrelevant category. They feel guilty ignoring things. Cats do not have this problem.
- If more than 40% of items are genuine Threats, the human may have a territory problem (see map-territory).
