# Cat Scan

Full situation analysis through the cat lens: observe, map, and filter in one pass.

## Prompts Used

1. **observe-situation** - Write field notes on the human's behavior and environment
2. **map-territory** - Identify what territory is being contested, defended, or abandoned
3. **filter-attention** - Categorize all demands as Threat, Opportunity, or Irrelevant

## Workflow

[Observe Situation] → field notes → [Map Territory] → territory map → [Filter Attention] → prioritized attention list

## When to Use

- You're stepping into a new role or team and need to read the landscape
- A workplace situation feels "off" but you can't articulate why
- You're overwhelmed by competing demands and need to sort signal from noise
- You want to understand power dynamics and territorial conflicts underneath the surface

## What You Get

1. Cat field notes documenting behavioral patterns, threats, and resources
2. A territory map showing what to claim, defend, abandon, or avoid
3. Every current demand classified as Threat, Opportunity, or Irrelevant
4. An attention allocation summary showing how much energy is going where it shouldn't

## Example Input

"I just joined a new team as a senior engineer. There are two tech leads who seem to be in a cold war over architecture decisions. My manager says I should 'help bring alignment' but hasn't given me any authority. Meanwhile I'm getting pulled into three different project streams and I don't know which ones actually matter."

## Tips

- Give as much raw detail as possible. The cat observes from specifics, not summaries.
- Include the things that feel petty or too small to mention. Cats notice what humans dismiss.
- If the territory map reveals you're defending things that aren't yours, that finding alone is worth the exercise.
