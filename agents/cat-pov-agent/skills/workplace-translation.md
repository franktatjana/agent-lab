# Workplace Translation

Full cat perspective with concrete next steps: observe the situation, prescribe action, then translate into workplace language.

## Prompts Used

1. **observe-situation** - Write cat field notes on behavior and environment
2. **prescribe-action** - Prescribe what to ignore, defend, rest from, and engage with
3. **translate-human** - Convert cat prescriptions into actionable workplace behavior

## Workflow

[Observe Situation] → field notes → [Prescribe Action] → cat prescription → [Translate Human] → action plan with scripts and sequencing

## When to Use

- You want the full cat reframe AND you need to walk into work Monday with a plan
- A specific situation needs both fresh perspective and practical response
- You've been stuck in your own framing and need distance before you can act
- You want to understand what's really happening and then do something about it

## What You Get

1. Cat field notes exposing patterns you've rationalized away
2. A direct prescription: what to ignore, defend, rest from, and engage with
3. Each prescription translated into specific workplace behaviors
4. Scripts and phrases for declining, defending, and communicating boundaries
5. A sequenced action plan: this week, this month, ongoing
6. Risk flags where the cat's advice needs social awareness adjustments

## Example Input

"My skip-level manager keeps scheduling 'check-ins' with me where she asks detailed questions about my team's technical decisions. My direct manager doesn't know about these meetings. I feel like I'm being managed around, but I'm not sure if I'm reading too much into it. I don't want to seem paranoid by bringing it up, but it's making me uncomfortable and I'm spending a lot of time preparing for these calls."

## Tips

- This is the longest skill, give it detailed input to work with.
- The observation phase often reveals that the real problem isn't what the human thinks it is. Trust the cat's read before jumping to solutions.
- Pay close attention to the risk mitigation section. The cat's advice is honest, but workplaces require diplomacy. The translation handles that gap.
