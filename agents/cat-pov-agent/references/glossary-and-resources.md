# Glossary and Resources

## Key Terms

- **Territory**: Your scope of ownership at work, the projects, decisions, and relationships that are yours to maintain and defend. Unclear territory leads to conflict and overwork.
- **Selective Attention**: The practice of categorizing incoming demands as Threat, Opportunity, or Irrelevant, then allocating energy accordingly. The core cat skill applied to workplace noise.
- **Hunt-to-Groom Ratio**: The proportion of your day spent on high-value output (hunting) versus low-value maintenance (grooming). If grooming dominates, your calendar owns you, not the other way around.
- **Cat Scan**: A diagnostic review of a human's work patterns through the cat lens, mapping where time goes, what gets attention, where energy leaks, and what territory is undefended or over-defended.
- **Boundary Hiss**: The clear, unmistakable signal that someone has crossed into your territory. Not aggressive, not personal, just a firm indication that this space is occupied. The professional equivalent: saying no without apologizing.
- **The Warm Spot**: The optimized environment where you do your best work, your ideal workspace, schedule structure, tool setup, and energy conditions. Cats find theirs instinctively. Humans need to design theirs deliberately.
- **Face Time vs. Hunt Time**: Face time is being visible without producing value. Hunt time is focused, output-driven work. Most humans over-index on face time because it feels like work without requiring the energy of actual work.
- **Nap Strategy**: Deliberate rest scheduled between periods of focused effort. Not reward for productivity, but preparation for the next productive cycle. Based on ultradian rhythms and attention restoration research.

## Related Agents

- **Corporate Navigator Agent**: When territorial dynamics involve organizational politics, power structures, or stakeholder management beyond individual boundaries
- **Leadership Coach Agent**: When the cat's observations point to energy management, boundary-setting, or delegation patterns that need sustained coaching
- **Why Agent**: When the human's behavior patterns have deeper root causes that surface-level observation cannot reach, the "why behind the why"
