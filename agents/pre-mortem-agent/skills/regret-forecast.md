# Regret Forecast

Projects forward 6-12 months from the decision point to identify what the team will wish they had considered, investigated, or acted on before committing.

## Prompts Used

1. **assume-failure** - Establish the failure context and surface the gap between plan and reality
2. **regret-projection** - Project forward to identify specific regret statements anchored in the decision
3. **mitigation-brief** - Propose mitigations for the highest-regret unconsidered factors

## Workflow

[Assume Failure] -> failure context -> [Regret Projection] -> specific regret inventory with reversibility assessment -> [Mitigation Brief] -> pre-commitment actions to reduce future regret

## When to Use

- The team is about to make an irreversible or difficult-to-reverse decision (acquisition, market exit, platform migration, major hire)
- Resources are being committed and alternatives are being foreclosed
- The team has not articulated what information they wish they had before deciding
- A "one more week" of investigation could materially change the quality of the decision
- The team is moving fast and needs a structured pause to check what they are skipping

## What You Get

1. A failure context establishing what the world looks like when the decision goes wrong
2. 3-5 specific regret statements: "We wish we had [action] before [commitment], because [consequence]"
3. A reversibility assessment for each regret: can the team course-correct later, or is this locked in?
4. The "one more week" recommendation: the single highest-leverage pre-commitment investigation
5. Mitigations for the highest-regret items

## Example Input

"We are signing a 3-year exclusive partnership with a logistics provider to handle all fulfillment for our D2C brand. The contract includes volume commitments and early termination penalties. Our current provider is adequate but slow. The new partner promises 2-day delivery nationwide. We have done a 30-day pilot in one region. The contract signing is scheduled for next Friday."

## Tips

- Irreversible decisions deserve the most pre-mortem time. A reversible decision that goes wrong costs time. An irreversible decision that goes wrong costs options.
- The "one more week" question is the highest-leverage output. If the team had seven more days, what single investigation would most reduce regret? That investigation often takes 2 days, not 7.
- Regret projection works because it engages loss aversion. People are more motivated to avoid feeling regret than to pursue marginal gains. Use that asymmetry.
