# Manage Emotions

Self-regulation techniques for staying grounded when a conversation triggers your own emotional responses.

## When to Use

As part of conversation preparation, especially when the user identifies high emotional stakes or personal triggers.

## Prompt

Help the user build an emotional regulation toolkit for before, during, and after the conversation.

**1. Know Your Triggers**
Identify the specific words, tones, or behaviors that are likely to trigger a strong emotional response in you.
- What are your hot buttons in this specific situation?
- What happens in your body when you're triggered? (chest tightens, face flushes, voice rises)
- What's your default reaction? (fight back, shut down, over-explain, apologize prematurely)

**2. Pre-Conversation Grounding**
- Physical: 4-7-8 breathing (inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8) for 3 cycles
- Mental: remind yourself of your intention, not your position
- Emotional: name your current emotional state before walking in

**3. In-Conversation Regulation**
When you notice a trigger:
- **Pause**: "Let me take a moment to think about that."
- **Name it internally**: "I'm feeling defensive right now."
- **Ground physically**: feet flat on floor, unclench hands, drop shoulders
- **Buy time**: "That's an important point. Can I respond to that in a moment?"
- **Take a break**: "I want to give this the attention it deserves. Can we pause for 5 minutes?"

**4. After the Conversation**
- Debrief with someone you trust (not a co-worker who will take sides)
- Write down what went well and what you'd do differently
- Give yourself permission to feel whatever you feel
- Don't replay and re-judge, process and release

Deliver as a personalized regulation plan based on the user's identified triggers and patterns.

## Tips

- Self-regulation is not suppression. It's staying present enough to choose your response instead of reacting.
- The break is always available. No conversation is so urgent it can't pause for 5 minutes.
