# Prepare Responses

Anticipate likely reactions and prepare grounded responses for each.

## When to Use

After the conversation plan is set. This prompt war-games the conversation by preparing for the most likely emotional responses.

## Prompt

For each likely reaction, prepare a grounded response. The goal is not to "win" the reaction, but to stay present and keep the conversation constructive.

**1. Anger / Blame**
If they respond with anger or turn it back on you:
- What they might say
- What it means (usually fear or hurt underneath)
- Your response: acknowledge the emotion, don't match the energy
- Example: "I can see this is frustrating. That's not my intention. Can you help me understand what's bothering you most?"

**2. Shutdown / Withdrawal**
If they go quiet, give one-word answers, or say "it's fine":
- What it means (usually overwhelm or self-protection)
- Your response: name the withdrawal, give space, don't fill silence
- Example: "I notice you've gone quiet. That's okay. We don't have to resolve everything now."

**3. Deflection / Topic Change**
If they redirect to a different grievance or minimize:
- What it means (usually avoiding the identity threat)
- Your response: acknowledge the deflection gently, redirect
- Example: "That's important too, and I want to discuss it. Right now I'd like to stay with [topic]."

**4. Tears / Emotional Overwhelm**
If they become visibly upset:
- Your response: pause, offer water/break, don't rush past it
- Example: "Take whatever time you need. This matters, and I don't want to rush through it."

**5. Agreement / People-Pleasing**
If they agree too quickly without genuine engagement:
- What it means (compliance is not resolution)
- Your response: test the agreement
- Example: "I appreciate you hearing me. Can you tell me in your own words what you're taking away from this?"

Deliver as a response card: scenario, likely meaning, prepared response.

## Tips

- Rehearse responses out loud. Reading them silently doesn't prepare your nervous system.
- The pause is the most powerful tool. When in doubt, say nothing for 3 seconds.
