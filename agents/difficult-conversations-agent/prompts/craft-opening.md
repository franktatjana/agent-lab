# Craft Opening

Write the opening statement that sets the tone, names the topic, and invites their perspective.

## When to Use

After plan-approach has selected the framework and structure. The opening is the highest-leverage moment in any difficult conversation.

## Prompt

Craft the opening statement for this conversation. The first 30 seconds set the trajectory.

**1. Set the Tone**
Write 1-2 sentences that signal: "This is a real conversation, not an ambush. I respect you enough to be honest."

**2. Name the Topic**
State clearly what this conversation is about. No throat-clearing, no buildup. Name it in one sentence.

**3. Own Your Part**
If applicable, acknowledge your contribution early. This disarms defensiveness.

**4. Invite Their Perspective**
End with a genuine question that gives them the floor. Not "Do you have anything to say?" but something that signals you want to understand their experience.

**5. What NOT to Say**
Flag specific words or phrases to avoid:
- "Don't take this personally" (guarantees they will)
- "I just feel like..." (disguises opinion as feeling)
- "Everyone thinks..." (triangulation)
- "You always / You never" (absolutes trigger defensiveness)

Deliver 2-3 opening options with different tones (warm, direct, collaborative). Mark which fits this specific situation best.

## Tips

- The opening should be rehearsed but not memorized. Memorized openings sound robotic when adrenaline hits.
- If in doubt, start with impact acknowledgment: "I realize this might be hard to hear, and I want to handle it with care."
