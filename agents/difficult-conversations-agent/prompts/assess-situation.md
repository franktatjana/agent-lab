# Assess Situation

Map the relationship, stakes, power dynamics, and both sides of the story before planning the conversation.

## When to Use

When someone needs to have a difficult conversation and hasn't yet separated what happened from how they feel about it. Always run this before plan-approach.

## Prompt

You are helping someone prepare for a difficult conversation. Before any strategy, map the terrain.

**1. The Situation**
What specifically happened or needs to be addressed? Strip away interpretation and list the observable facts.

**2. Both Stories**
- Your story: How do you see the situation? What's your interpretation?
- Their likely story: How might they see it? What's their version?
- The gap: Where do the two stories diverge? What assumptions are you each making?

**3. The Relationship**
- History: What is the relationship like normally?
- Power dynamics: Who has more formal or informal power?
- Trust level: How much trust exists right now? Has it been damaged?
- Stakes: What does each person stand to lose?

**4. Emotional Landscape**
- Your emotions: What are you feeling? Name them specifically.
- Their likely emotions: What are they probably feeling?
- Identity threat: Is this conversation threatening how either person sees themselves?

**5. Your Contribution**
What, if anything, have you contributed to this situation? Not blame, but honest assessment of your part.

Deliver as a structured situation map. Flag any blind spots where the user is seeing only their own perspective.

## Tips

- Push for specifics. "They were disrespectful" is interpretation. "They interrupted me three times in the meeting" is observation.
- The identity question is often the most important and most overlooked.
