# In-Conversation Toolkit

Pre-built responses for likely reactions plus self-regulation techniques for staying grounded.

## Prompts Used

1. **prepare-responses** - War-game likely reactions with grounded responses
2. **manage-emotions** - Build personal emotional regulation toolkit

## Workflow

[Prepare Responses] → response cards → [Manage Emotions] → regulation plan

## When to Use

- When you know the conversation will be emotionally charged
- When the other person tends to react with anger, tears, or shutdown
- When you have a history of losing composure in conflict

## What You'll Get

1. Response cards for 5 common reaction types (anger, shutdown, deflection, tears, quick agreement)
2. Your personal trigger map (what sets you off and how you know it's happening)
3. In-conversation grounding techniques
4. Break protocols (when and how to pause)
5. Post-conversation debrief plan

## Example Input

"My co-founder gets aggressive when challenged. Last time I raised concerns about our burn rate, he accused me of not believing in the vision. I shut down and dropped it."

## Tips

- Rehearse responses out loud. Silent reading doesn't prepare your nervous system.
- The toolkit is a safety net, not a playbook. Use what you need in the moment.
