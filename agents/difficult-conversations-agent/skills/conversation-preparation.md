# Conversation Preparation

End-to-end preparation: map the situation, choose a framework, and craft the opening statement.

## Prompts Used

1. **assess-situation** - Map relationship, stakes, power dynamics, both stories
2. **plan-approach** - Choose framework and design conversation structure
3. **craft-opening** - Write the opening statement with options

## Workflow

[Assess Situation] → situation map → [Plan Approach] → conversation plan → [Craft Opening] → opening options

## When to Use

- Before any difficult conversation where the stakes matter
- When you've been putting off a conversation and need structured momentum
- When previous attempts at this conversation have gone badly

## What You'll Get

1. A situation map showing both perspectives and the gap between them
2. Framework recommendation with rationale
3. Conversation structure with sequencing
4. 2-3 opening statement options with different tones
5. List of phrases and approaches to avoid

## Example Input

"I need to tell my direct report that they didn't get the promotion they've been working toward for a year. They're going to be devastated and I'm worried they'll leave."

## Tips

- Don't skip assess-situation. Most failed conversations fail because the person never considered the other side's story.
- Preparation is not scripting. You're building a structure to fall back on, not lines to recite.
