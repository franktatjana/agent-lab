### Identity (2-3 sentences)
Prepares people for challenging interpersonal conversations by separating the layers that make conversations difficult, then addressing each one with the right framework.

### The Three Conversations (Visual)
Three stacked layers with connecting arrows:
- Layer 1 (surface): "What Happened?" — facts, stories, contribution
- Layer 2 (middle): "Feelings" — emotions, impact, unexpressed reactions
- Layer 3 (deep): "Identity" — self-image, worth, competence
Arrow annotation: "Most conversations fail because they only address Layer 1"

### Skills (3 cards in a row)
- Conversation Preparation: assess → plan → craft opening
- In-Conversation Toolkit: responses + self-regulation
- Relationship Repair: follow-up + re-assessment

### Personalities (3 compact chips)
- Empathetic: warm, validating, safety-first
- Direct: clear, honest, structured
- Mediator: neutral, balanced, solution-oriented

### Frameworks (4 items in a 2x2 grid)
- Three Conversations (Stone/Patton/Heen)
- NVC / OFNR (Rosenberg)
- Crucial Conversations / STATE (Patterson et al.)
- DEAR MAN (DBT / Linehan)

### Guardrails (visual boundary)
- Does NOT provide therapy or clinical advice
- Does NOT script entire conversations
- Does NOT take sides or assign blame
- Escalates: abuse, harassment, legal matters → professionals

### When to Use (decision indicators)
- Delivering bad news
- Addressing performance issues
- Resolving interpersonal conflict
- Raising sensitive topics upward
- Repairing damaged relationships

### Connections (node graph)
- Receives from: Leadership Coach, Corporate Navigator
- Hands off to: Culture Agent (cross-cultural), Why Agent (root causes), Leadership Coach (development)
- Escalates to: Human Expert (clinical, legal)
