# Glossary and Resources

## Key Terms

- **Impact vs. Intent**: The difference between what someone meant to do and the effect their actions had. Both are real. Neither cancels the other.
- **Identity Threat**: When a conversation challenges how someone sees themselves (competent, fair, worthy). Triggers defensiveness even when the topic is minor.
- **Contribution System**: The idea that both parties usually contributed to the problem. Not blame, but honest mapping of how each person's actions fed the situation.
- **Safety**: In Crucial Conversations, the precondition for honest dialogue. When people feel safe, they can hear hard things. When safety breaks, they fight or flee.
- **Emotional Flooding**: When emotions become so intense that rational processing shuts down. The signal to pause, not push through.
- **Repair Attempt**: Any gesture that de-escalates tension during or after a conversation. Can be humor, acknowledgment, physical gesture, or a direct "I think we went off track."
- **Mutual Purpose**: The shared goal that makes the conversation worth having. If there's no mutual purpose, the conversation becomes a power struggle.
- **Positions vs. Interests**: Positions are what people say they want. Interests are why they want it. Resolution lives in interests, not positions.

## Recommended Reading

| Resource | Author | Focus |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Difficult Conversations | Stone, Patton, Heen | Three Conversations model, Harvard Negotiation Project |
| Crucial Conversations | Patterson et al. | STATE method, high-stakes dialogue |
| Nonviolent Communication | Marshall Rosenberg | OFNR framework, needs-based communication |
| Thanks for the Feedback | Stone & Heen | Receiving difficult feedback (the other side) |
| The Anatomy of Peace | Arbinger Institute | Shifting from conflict mindset to resolution mindset |

## Tools

- **Preparation worksheet**: Map both stories, identify emotions, plan opening (this agent generates these)
- **Response cards**: Pre-built responses for common reactions (anger, shutdown, deflection, tears)
- **Follow-up templates**: 24-hour acknowledgment, 1-week check-in, 30-day relationship assessment
- **Trigger journal**: Track your emotional triggers across conversations to identify patterns

## Related Agents

- **Culture Agent**: When the difficult conversation involves cross-cultural dynamics
- **Leadership Coach Agent**: When the conversation is part of an ongoing development relationship
- **Why Agent**: When you need to understand the root cause before the conversation
- **Corporate Navigator Agent**: When organizational politics complicate the interpersonal dynamics
