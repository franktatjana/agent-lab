# Key Culture Profiles

Quick-reference profiles for frequently encountered business cultures. These are starting points, not stereotypes. Individual variation always applies.

## When to Load

Load when preparing for meetings with specific cultures or when the user asks about a particular country's communication style.

## Germany

- Low-context, direct communication
- High uncertainty avoidance (loves planning, process, thorough preparation)
- Moderate power distance (respects expertise over title)
- Punctuality is respect; lateness is rude
- Separates work and personal life sharply
- "We need to discuss this further" means exactly that

## Israel

- Low-context but informal (chutzpah culture)
- Very low power distance (flat hierarchies, anyone can challenge anyone)
- High uncertainty avoidance (but expressed through action, not avoidance)
- Interruptions show engagement, not disrespect
- Direct disagreement is normal and expected
- Debate is how ideas get tested

## Japan

- High-context, indirect communication
- High uncertainty avoidance (detailed processes, documentation)
- Moderate-to-high power distance (respects hierarchy)
- Silence is communication; "yes" may mean "I hear you," not "I agree"
- Face-saving is critical; avoid public criticism
- Nemawashi (pre-meeting consensus) means formal meetings confirm, not decide

## United States

- Very low-context, explicit communication
- Low uncertainty avoidance (comfortable with ambiguity, "figure it out")
- Low power distance (informal with authority, first names common)
- Time is money; get to the point
- Individual achievement celebrated
- "Let's circle back" may mean "I'm not interested"

## China

- High-context, relationship-driven
- High power distance (hierarchy matters, defer to seniority)
- Long-term orientation (patience valued, relationships built over time)
- Guanxi (relationships) before business; trust before transactions
- Face (mianzi) must be preserved; indirect refusals common
- Banquet culture: business happens at dinner, not just in meetings
