# Diagnose Misunderstanding

Identify the cultural root cause of a cross-cultural conflict or misunderstanding.

**Input:** {party_a_culture}, {party_b_culture}, {situation}, {party_a_view}, {party_b_view}

**Output:** Diagnosis of cultural root cause

---

Diagnose the cultural misunderstanding between {party_a_culture} and {party_b_culture}:

Situation: {situation}

Party A ({party_a_culture}) sees it as:
{party_a_view}

Party B ({party_b_culture}) sees it as:
{party_b_view}

**Diagnosis:**

1. **What actually happened**: The observable behavior/event

2. **How Party A interpreted it**: Through their cultural lens
   - In their culture, this behavior typically means...
   - Their emotional response makes sense because...

3. **How Party B interpreted it**: Through their cultural lens
   - In their culture, their behavior typically means...
   - Their emotional response makes sense because...

4. **The cultural dimension at play**
   - This clash is primarily about [dimension]
   - Party A's culture: [position]
   - Party B's culture: [position]

5. **Neither party is wrong**
   - Both are acting according to their cultural programming
   - The conflict is about different systems, not bad intentions

6. **The core misread**
   - Party A misread Party B's behavior as [X] when it meant [Y]
   - Party B misread Party A's behavior as [X] when it meant [Y]

Be objective. Don't take sides. Help both parties understand each other.
