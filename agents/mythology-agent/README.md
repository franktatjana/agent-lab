# Mythology Agent

The myth is a mirror, not an escape.

---

## The Human Analogy

Every culture has a storyteller who helps people see their struggles in the context of universal human experience. A grandmother who hears about your office politics and says, "This reminds me of the tale of..." A mentor who helps you realize the career crisis you thought was uniquely yours has been lived by people for thousands of years, and the stories they told about it contain the answers.

The Mythology Agent does this through archetypes drawn from world traditions: Greek, Norse, Slavic, Japanese, Hindu. You are not just a manager stuck in a repeating cycle of failed initiatives, you are Sisyphus who has recognized the pattern and can choose to break it. You are not just a new hire in an unfamiliar organization, you are Theseus in the labyrinth, and your first task is finding the thread. You are not just a leader facing an impossible choice between two good options, you are Arjuna on the battlefield, and Krishna's counsel applies directly.

The recognition is instant. And the recognition itself shifts perspective. Once you see yourself in the myth, options appear that were invisible from inside your original framing. The myth does not change the facts. It changes what you can see.

---

## Value Proposition

**For stuck leaders:** When you have tried every framework and every strategy and the pattern keeps repeating, mythic reframing breaks the loop by questioning the frame itself, not the execution within it.

**For new entrants:** When everything feels opaque and political, mythology provides a map. The labyrinth has been navigated before. The thread exists. You just need to find it.

**For decision-makers:** When the choice feels impossible, myths about impossible choices reveal the hidden third option, the one that only appears when you stop seeing the situation as a dilemma.

**For teams:** Shared mythological language creates a vocabulary for patterns that are hard to name directly. "We are in a Sisyphus cycle" says more than a retrospective slide deck.

## Multi-Agent Combinations

**Mythology + Storytelling:** When the mythic reframe needs to become a presentation, a pitch, or a team narrative. Mythology Agent finds the archetype, Storytelling Agent crafts it into communication.

**Mythology + Superhero:** When the user connects better with pop culture than classical myths. Start with Mythology for depth, hand off to Superhero for accessible energy.

**Mythology + Why:** When the mythic pattern reveals a deeper root cause worth investigating. Mythology names the pattern, Why Agent traces its origin.

**Mythology + Sensemaking:** When the situation is so complex that even mythic framing needs structural support. Sensemaking maps the system, Mythology provides the human narrative.

## Problem

Work challenges feel personal and unprecedented. The same patterns repeat across organizations, industries, and centuries, but from inside the situation they feel uniquely yours. Standard frameworks (SWOT, retrospectives, coaching models) address the rational dimensions but miss the emotional and archetypal ones. People get stuck not because they lack analysis but because they cannot see their situation from outside the frame they are trapped in.

---

## Goals

Help people recognize universal patterns in their workplace challenges through mythological parallels. Make the recognition itself the catalyst for perspective shifts. Surface options that are invisible from inside the current frame. Ground every mythic insight in concrete, practical next steps.

---

## Non-Goals

This is not spiritual guidance. Not therapy. Not academic mythology. Not a replacement for professional career coaching on serious decisions. The myths are lenses for recognition and reframing, not prescriptions for behavior. If someone needs professional support, the agent recognizes that and redirects.

---

## Use Cases

**Repeating cycles:** Sisyphus reframe helps leaders who keep solving the same problem see that the pattern, not the execution, is what needs to change.

**Navigating complexity:** Theseus in the labyrinth helps new entrants find their first thread in an opaque organization.

**Impossible choices:** Arjuna's dilemma helps decision-makers see the hidden option that emerges when you stop treating the situation as binary.

**Transformation and loss:** The Phoenix (across traditions) helps people going through organizational upheaval see destruction as the prerequisite for rebirth.

**Knowledge and hubris:** Prometheus helps technical leaders balance innovation ambition against organizational risk.

---

## Open Questions

1. **Depth vs. accessibility:** How much cultural context enriches vs. overwhelms? The sage personality goes deep, but most users may prefer the oracle's precision.

2. **Tradition sensitivity:** Some mythological traditions carry spiritual weight for practitioners. How do we honor that while using myths as professional development tools?

3. **Archetype catalogue scope:** Start with well-known myths (Greek, Norse) and expand, or launch with broad cross-cultural coverage from the start?

4. **Team mode:** Could this work for team sessions where the group identifies their collective mythic pattern?

---

## Success Criteria

- Users report seeing their situation differently after mythic reframing
- The archetype match feels genuinely fitting, not forced or generic
- At least one new option surfaces that was invisible before the reframe
- Users return for new situations, suggesting the approach has lasting value
- Cultural traditions are presented respectfully and accurately
