# Clarify Situation

Given conflicting signals or ambiguous context, construct a coherent picture of what's actually happening. This is the core sensemaking skill: collecting scattered, contradictory signals and synthesizing them into an assessment that is more complete than any individual stakeholder's view.

## Prompts Used

1. **gather-signals** - Collect and organize conflicting signals, narratives, and data points from the situation
2. **map-narratives** - Identify the different stories stakeholders are telling about what's happening
3. **synthesize-picture** - Combine signals and narratives into a coherent situation assessment

## Workflow

[Gather Signals] -> organized signal inventory -> [Map Narratives] -> stakeholder narrative map -> [Synthesize Picture] -> coherent situation assessment with competing narratives

## When to Use

- Multiple stakeholders describe the same situation differently and the team cannot agree on what is happening
- Data points contradict each other and the team does not know which to trust
- The situation keeps shifting and previous assessments feel stale or incomplete
- A new leader has joined and needs to understand a complex situation quickly
- The team has been operating in ambiguity for weeks and needs a structured pause to take stock

## What You Get

1. A structured signal inventory that separates facts from interpretations and identifies gaps
2. A narrative map showing 2-4 distinct stories stakeholders are telling, with signal coverage for each
3. A coherent situation assessment (3-5 sentences) that synthesizes the strongest signals across narratives
4. Pattern recognition that surfaces dynamics invisible from any single stakeholder's position

## Example Input

"Our enterprise sales team says the product is losing competitiveness because engineering ships too slowly. Engineering says they are blocked by constant priority changes from product. Product says leadership keeps changing strategy every quarter. The CEO says the strategy has been consistent but execution is the problem. Meanwhile, our two largest customers have both asked for a contract review meeting next month."

## Tips

- Run this skill when the team cannot agree on what the problem is. If the team agrees on the problem but disagrees on the solution, that is a different skill (Six Hats, Wargaming). Clarify-situation is for when the situation itself is contested.
- The narrative map is often the most surprising output. Teams rarely see how differently their colleagues interpret the same events.
- The synthesis should surprise at least one stakeholder. If everyone reads it and says "yes, I already knew that," the synthesis is too conservative. It should reveal a pattern that was invisible from any single position.
