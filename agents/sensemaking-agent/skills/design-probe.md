# Design Probe

For complex situations where analysis has reached its limits, design safe-to-fail experiments that generate learning through action. In complex domains, the system is adaptive and unpredictable, meaning the only way to understand it is to interact with it at small scale and observe what emerges.

## Prompts Used

1. **classify-cynefin** - Classify the domain to confirm the situation warrants probing rather than analysis
2. **design-experiment** - Design safe-to-fail probes that generate learning without requiring large commitments

## Workflow

[Classify Cynefin] -> domain classification confirming complexity -> [Design Experiment] -> portfolio of 2-4 safe-to-fail probes with observation plans and abort criteria

## When to Use

- The domain has been classified as complex and the team is stuck because more analysis produces more confusion, not more clarity
- The team is debating two fundamentally different approaches and neither has strong enough evidence to win the argument
- The situation involves a complex adaptive system (market, organization, ecosystem) where outcomes cannot be predicted from inputs
- Previous large-scale initiatives in similar situations have failed, and the team needs a lower-cost learning approach
- Time pressure makes a 6-month analysis cycle impractical, but the stakes are too high for a large-scale bet

## What You Get

1. A domain classification confirming that the situation is complex and warrants probing
2. A portfolio of 2-4 safe-to-fail probes, each with:
   - A clear hypothesis stated as "If [action], we expect [signal]; if [alternative signal], it means [implication]"
   - A concrete, time-bounded action executable within days
   - An observation plan with pre-defined success and failure signals
   - Abort criteria defining when to stop the probe
   - Cost in time, money, and political capital
3. A learning integration plan: what decision the probes will inform and what to do with convergent or divergent results

## Example Input

"We classified our Japanese market entry as complex. Our country manager and consultants disagree on whether the issue is our product positioning, our partnership approach, or our pricing model. We have budget for small experiments but not for another failed large-scale partnership attempt. We need to learn what's actually driving the unpredictable results before committing to a revised market entry strategy."

## Tips

- Safe-to-fail means the worst case is tolerable, not that failure is unlikely. Design probes expecting some to fail. The failures teach as much as the successes.
- Run probes in parallel, not sequence. Parallel probes produce faster learning and reduce the risk of confirmation bias (interpreting the first result and designing subsequent probes to confirm it).
- Political probes are legitimate. In organizational complexity, a conversation with a key stakeholder to test their reaction to a preliminary idea is a probe. The observation is their response. The learning is how the organizational system reacts.
- The abort criteria are as important as the probe design. A probe without abort criteria is not safe to fail because the team will not know when to stop investing in a failing experiment.
