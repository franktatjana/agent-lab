# Build Scenarios

Construct four distinct, plausible future narratives from critical uncertainties. Take two uncertainty axes and fill each quadrant of the 2x2 matrix with a world that has its own internal logic, dynamics, and implications. The output is a set of four named scenarios that span the space of plausible futures and provide the test environments for wind tunneling.

## Prompts Used

1. **rank-uncertainties** - Confirm or refine the two critical uncertainty axes that will form the matrix
2. **construct-worlds** - Cross the two uncertainties to build four distinct scenario narratives with internal logic
3. **name-scenarios** - Give each scenario a memorable, evocative name and a 2-3 sentence capsule narrative

## Workflow

[Rank Uncertainties] -> confirmed axes with endpoints -> [Construct Worlds] -> four scenario narratives with settings, mechanisms, and internal logic -> [Name Scenarios] -> four named scenarios with capsule narratives and 2x2 matrix placement

## When to Use

- The team has identified critical uncertainties and needs to construct the scenario set for strategic planning
- An existing set of scenarios feels stale and needs to be refreshed with updated uncertainty axes
- A new strategic question requires fresh scenarios built from the question's specific uncertainty landscape
- The team needs a shared vocabulary for discussing alternative futures, which named scenarios provide
- A planning offsite or strategy workshop needs a scenario set as the foundation for wind tunneling

## What You Get

1. Validated uncertainty axes with clear endpoint descriptions and independence confirmation
2. Four scenario worlds, each with setting, mechanism, winners and losers, pace and character, and internal logic
3. A distinctiveness check confirming the four worlds are genuinely different, not variations of a theme
4. Background conditions that are common across all four scenarios
5. Four memorable names with 2-3 sentence capsule narratives
6. A labeled 2x2 matrix showing the placement of each named scenario

## Example Input

"Critical uncertainties: (1) AI capability trajectory, from 'AI capabilities centralize in 3-5 mega-providers' to 'AI capabilities distribute across thousands of specialized models.' (2) Healthcare regulation trajectory, from 'prescriptive compliance regime requiring pre-approval for all AI-assisted clinical decisions' to 'outcome-based regulatory framework that certifies results, not methods.' Time horizon: 5 years. Strategic context: mid-market SaaS company pivoting to vertical healthcare solutions."

## Tips

- Internal logic is the quality test. Each scenario should be internally consistent: the conditions, the behaviors, the winners and losers should all follow logically from how the two uncertainties resolved. If a team member can ask "but in this world, would X really happen?" and the answer is "yes, because of Y," the scenario has good internal logic.
- The naming step is not cosmetic. Teams that cannot remember scenario names cannot use scenarios in daily decisions. Spend time on names. They are the organizational handles for future reference.
- All four scenarios should feel equally developed. If one scenario gets a rich narrative and another gets a brief sketch, the team will unconsciously weight the richer scenario as more important. Balance the development across all four.
- Resist making one scenario the "good" future. Every scenario should have opportunities and threats. A world with heavy regulation may constrain innovation speed but may also create barriers to entry that benefit incumbents.
