# Identify Uncertainties

Surface the critical unknowns that will shape the future landscape. Scan broadly across driving forces, then narrow to the two axes that matter most for the strategic question. This is the foundation skill: the quality of every downstream step, scenario construction, wind tunneling, signpost identification, depends on selecting the right uncertainties.

## Prompts Used

1. **scan-environment** - Cast a wide net across PESTEL dimensions and domain-specific forces to build a driving forces inventory
2. **rank-uncertainties** - From the driving forces, select the two that are highest-impact and most genuinely uncertain to serve as scenario axes

## Workflow

[Scan Environment] -> driving forces inventory with direction, momentum, uncertainty ratings -> [Rank Uncertainties] -> two critical uncertainty axes with rationale, endpoints, and independence validation

## When to Use

- A team is entering a new market or facing a major strategic decision and needs to understand which uncertainties will shape the outcome
- The organization has been planning from the same assumptions for years and needs to check whether those assumptions still hold
- Leadership wants to understand what they do not know before committing resources to a strategic direction
- The team is about to build scenarios but has not yet identified the axes, skipping this step produces scenarios that are either too obvious or too speculative
- An industry is experiencing rapid change and the team needs to separate genuine uncertainties from well-understood trends

## What You Get

1. A structured inventory of driving forces across political, economic, social, technological, environmental, and legal dimensions
2. Domain-specific forces relevant to the strategic context: competitive dynamics, value chain shifts, customer evolution
3. Force interactions that identify where combinations of forces could produce discontinuities
4. Two critical uncertainty axes with high-impact rationale, genuine uncertainty evidence, endpoint descriptions, and independence validation

## Example Input

"We are a mid-market SaaS company considering a major pivot from horizontal CRM to vertical healthcare solutions. We have $15M in ARR, 200 employees, and 3 years of runway. The CEO believes healthcare digitization is a generational opportunity. The board is split on whether to bet the company on this vertical. Time horizon: 5 years."

## Tips

- The scan should be broader than the team expects. The most important uncertainties for a healthcare SaaS pivot may not be in healthcare technology at all: they may be in regulatory policy, reimbursement model changes, or AI capability trajectories that reshape the competitive landscape.
- Good uncertainty axes feel uncomfortable. If the team immediately agrees that both axes are "the right ones," they may be picking comfortable, well-understood forces rather than genuinely uncertain ones. Push for axes where reasonable, informed people disagree on direction.
- Independence between axes is non-negotiable. If the two axes are correlated, the 2x2 matrix collapses into a 1x2 with one plausible and one implausible row. Test independence before proceeding to scenario construction.
