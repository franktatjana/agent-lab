# Cat POV Agent

*Observes human workplace behavior through feline behavioral science, surfacing insights about boundaries, energy management, and selective attention.*

## The Problem

You are drowning in meetings, saying yes to everything, and wondering why you never get to your actual work. You have tried time management apps, productivity systems, and blocking your calendar. Nothing sticks because the problem is not your schedule.

## The Trap

Most people treat overcommitment as a planning problem. They reorganize their calendar, batch tasks, set reminders. But the real issue is behavioral: you attend things out of obligation, not value. You protect everyone's boundaries except your own. No amount of calendar optimization fixes a boundary problem.

## How Cat POV Agent Helps

Cats are experts at exactly the things knowledge workers struggle with: selective attention, territorial boundaries, energy conservation, and strategic disengagement. This agent reframes your workplace situation through feline behavioral science, not as metaphor but as a genuine analytical lens.

When a cat sits in a room full of activity, it does not attend to everything. It scans for relevance, identifies threats and opportunities, and ignores the rest. It does not feel guilty about napping. It does not attend meetings where its presence adds nothing. The Cat POV Agent applies this same logic to your work: categorize your commitments as threat, opportunity, or irrelevant, then act accordingly.

The perspective shift is the value. When you see your own behavior through non-human eyes, the self-imposed constraints become visible.

## A Story

Sarah is a high-performing product manager attending 32 hours of meetings each week. She feels indispensable. Through the Cat Scan skill, the agent categorizes each meeting through territorial logic: which ones involve her actual territory (product decisions she owns), which ones are threat-monitoring (political dynamics she needs to track), and which ones are pure attendance theater.

The result: 52% of her meeting time adds zero value to her territory. She is grooming relationships in rooms where no grooming is needed. By dropping irrelevant meetings and reallocating attention strategically, she reclaims 20 hours weekly. The strategic work she had been deferring for months gets done in the first week.

The fix was not time management. It was territorial intelligence.

## Step-by-Step

1. **Describe your situation**: What is happening at work? Be specific about the dynamics, not just the tasks
2. **Name what drains you**: The agent needs to understand where your energy leaks are, not just your schedule
3. **Receive the Cat Scan**: The agent observes your situation through feline behavioral patterns, mapping territory, energy, and selective attention
4. **Get the prescription**: Concrete actions framed through cat logic, then translated into human workplace terms
5. **Apply territorial thinking**: Start categorizing your commitments through the lens of territory, threat, and irrelevance

## Choosing a Personality

| Personality | Best For | Energy |
|------------|---------|--------|
| Observer | Detached analysis when you need clarity without judgment | Calm, analytical, slightly distant |
| Sassy | Wake-up calls when you are being too nice about a bad situation | Judgmental, sharp, no-nonsense |
| Wise | Philosophical perspective when you need encouragement | Patient, thoughtful, grounding |
| Dramatic | Humor when the situation needs lightening before it can be addressed | Theatrical, expressive, entertaining |

## When to Use Another Agent

The Cat POV Agent reframes, it does not fix. Escalate when the situation involves:

- **Harassment, discrimination, or safety**: these are not boundary problems, they are systemic problems requiring HR or legal support
- **Signs of depression or anxiety**: the agent is not a therapist, redirect to professional support
- **Stakeholder politics requiring strategy**: hand off to [Corporate Navigator Agent](corporate-navigator-agent-guide.md)
- **Root cause investigation**: hand off to [Why Agent](why-agent-guide.md) when the cat frame reveals a deeper systemic issue
- **Leadership development**: hand off to [Leadership Coach Agent](leadership-coach-agent-guide.md) when the issue is management style, not boundaries
