# Difficult Conversations Agent

*Prepares people for challenging interpersonal conversations by separating the layers that make conversations difficult, then addressing each one.*

## The Problem

You need to deliver feedback that will hurt someone. Or you need to address behavior that is affecting the team. Or you need to tell someone they did not get the promotion. The stakes feel high because the relationship matters and you cannot predict how they will react. So you rehearse in your head, over-prepare the facts, and hope the right words come to you in the moment.

## The Trap

Most people prepare for difficult conversations by building their case: gathering evidence, rehearsing arguments, preparing counterpoints. This creates a prosecutorial mindset, optimized for being right rather than being heard. When you walk in armed with facts, the other person experiences it as an attack, not a conversation. They defend, you escalate, and the relationship takes damage regardless of who "wins."

The deeper trap: treating it as one conversation when it is actually three.

## How Difficult Conversations Agent Helps

This agent uses the Three Conversations framework (from the Harvard Negotiation Project): every difficult conversation is actually three simultaneous conversations happening at once.

**The "What Happened" conversation**: the facts, events, and actions. Both parties have different versions. Neither version is complete.

**The Feelings conversation**: emotions on both sides that are shaping interpretation. These are data, not obstacles.

**The Identity conversation**: what this situation means about who you are as a person. Identity threat ("Am I competent? Am I a good person?") drives most defensive reactions.

Preparing for all three, not just the factual layer, changes the outcome entirely. The agent maps your situation from both perspectives, helps you own your contribution (not blame, contribution), prepares opening statements, and builds response plans for likely reactions.

## A Story

Marcus is an engineering director who needs to tell his best engineer, Priya, that she did not get the promotion she worked toward for 18 months. His first attempt goes badly. He leads with organizational reasons ("the committee felt..."), which Priya hears as dismissal. Within 48 hours, she updates LinkedIn.

Marcus comes to the Difficult Conversations Agent for the repair conversation. The agent maps the three layers:

- **What Happened**: Priya was not promoted. But Marcus also failed to advocate as strongly as he should have, and the criteria were not transparent
- **Feelings**: Priya feels betrayed and undervalued. Marcus feels guilty and defensive
- **Identity**: Priya's identity as "the best engineer" is threatened. Marcus's identity as "a good leader" is threatened

The agent helps Marcus prepare an opening that acknowledges all three: "I owe you an honest conversation about what happened and where I fell short." He owns his contribution (inadequate advocacy) before explaining the committee's reasoning. He names the identity threat directly: "Your technical excellence is not in question, and I understand that this decision makes it feel like it is."

Priya does not leave. Nine months later, she gets the promotion. The pivot: Marcus shifted from defending a decision to owning his part in it.

## Step-by-Step

1. **Describe the situation**: What happened, what needs to be said, what is at stake
2. **Map both perspectives**: The agent helps you see the situation from the other person's view, not just yours
3. **Separate the three conversations**: Facts, feelings, and identity threats are addressed individually
4. **Own your contribution**: Not blame, but honest accounting of your part in the dynamic
5. **Prepare the opening**: A specific opening statement that sets the right tone
6. **Anticipate reactions**: The agent prepares response plans for likely defensive reactions
7. **Plan the follow-up**: The conversation does not end when you leave the room

## Choosing a Personality

| Personality | Best For | Energy |
|------------|---------|--------|
| Empathetic | Sensitive situations, fragile trust, high emotional stakes | Warm, careful, validates feelings first |
| Direct | Time-sensitive feedback, situations where clarity is kindness | Clear, honest, no-nonsense framing |
| Mediator | Conflict resolution where neither party is clearly right | Balanced, sees both sides, finds common ground |

## When to Use Another Agent

The Difficult Conversations Agent prepares for interpersonal exchanges, it does not handle every workplace challenge. Escalate when:

- **Abuse, harassment, or legal matters**: these require professional HR or legal support, not conversation prep
- **Clinical mental health concerns**: redirect to professional support
- **Cross-cultural friction** is the primary factor: hand off to [Culture Agent](culture-agent-guide.md)
- **Stakeholder politics** are driving the conflict: hand off to [Corporate Navigator Agent](corporate-navigator-agent-guide.md)
- **Root cause investigation** needed before conversation: hand off to [Why Agent](why-agent-guide.md)
