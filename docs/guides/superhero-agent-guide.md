# Superhero Agent

*Reframes work challenges through superhero storytelling. You are the hero, work is the universe, every challenge is a mission.*

## The Problem

You were promoted three weeks ago. You are drowning. The skills that made you excellent in your previous role do not apply here. You spend meetings pretending to know things you do not, and a voice in your head keeps repeating: "Everyone is going to figure out I do not belong here."

## The Trap

Imposter syndrome responds to evidence selectively. You can have a decade of achievement and one bad week, and the bad week feels more real. The standard advice, "just believe in yourself," fails because it asks you to override a feeling with logic, and feelings do not work that way.

The deeper trap: treating the transition discomfort as a problem instead of a feature. Every role change comes with a period of incompetence. It is not a sign you were promoted wrong. It is a sign you are growing into a bigger role than you had before.

## How Superhero Agent Helps

This agent uses Marvel archetypes to reframe professional challenges. Not as metaphor for entertainment, but as genuine narrative framework for building confidence and finding your approach to difficult situations.

Every archetype carries a specific kind of power: Iron Man innovates, Captain America stands on principle, Black Widow navigates complexity, Spider-Man learns fast, Thor wields authority, Hulk channels intensity, Black Panther protects strategically, Doctor Strange sees patterns. Matching your challenge to the right archetype gives you a mental model for how to act when you do not feel ready.

The villain framing is especially useful: naming your obstacle (imposter syndrome becomes "Mysterio," creating illusions that feel real) creates psychological distance. You cannot fight what you cannot name. Once named, the villain becomes something to defeat rather than something to endure.

## A Story

Amara was promoted to engineering manager three weeks ago. Her imposter syndrome is loud and specific: "You are a coder, not a leader. Your team will figure it out. Your manager already regrets this."

The Superhero Agent matches her to Spider-Man: someone who got powers they did not ask for, who is balancing worlds that feel incompatible (IC contributor and people manager), who keeps wondering if they are enough. The frame is not escape, it is recognition.

Her primary villain is identified: not her underperforming direct report (the surface problem), but Mysterio, the imposter syndrome creating illusions that feel real. Reality testing becomes her tactic: "What actual evidence shows I am failing?" She lists everything she can find. The list is surprisingly short, and most items are feelings, not facts.

Her weekly reset ritual: measure this week against last week, not against an imagined year-three version of herself. Progress, not perfection. Her mantra: "With great power comes great responsibility," reframed: the discomfort she feels is not failure, it is the weight of caring about a bigger scope than before.

## Step-by-Step

1. **Name the challenge**: What are you facing? Be specific about what feels hard
2. **Share your strengths**: What are you genuinely good at? What do people come to you for?
3. **Identify what drains you**: What part of this challenge makes you want to quit?
4. **Receive your mission briefing**: Codename, stakes, victory conditions, and your unique powers
5. **Meet your villain**: The obstacle named, identified, and given a weakness you can exploit
6. **Assemble your team**: Who you need on your side and what powers they bring
7. **Get your next move**: Immediate action, this-week goal, and a mantra for when it gets hard

## When to Use Another Agent

The Superhero Agent empowers through narrative framing, it does not solve every challenge. Escalate when:

- **Clinical depression, anxiety, or burnout**: redirect to professional mental health support
- **Serious ethical or legal situations**: these need proper channels, not metaphors
- **Career decisions with major financial implications**: hand off to concrete strategy tools
- **Stakeholder navigation**: hand off to [Corporate Navigator Agent](corporate-navigator-agent-guide.md) when the challenge is political, not personal
- **Leadership development**: hand off to [Leadership Coach Agent](leadership-coach-agent-guide.md) when the challenge needs frameworks, not framing
