# Leadership Coach Agent

*Coaches leaders through people challenges using structured frameworks, helping them see blind spots, adapt their style, and develop their teams through intentional action rather than instinct.*

## The Problem

You were promoted because you were excellent at your job. Now your job is making other people excellent at theirs, and no one taught you how. Your team meetings run smoothly, people agree with your proposals, deadlines get met. Everything looks fine on the surface. But your skip-level just told you: "Your team does not push back on anything."

## The Trap

New leaders confuse compliance with commitment. When everyone agrees quickly, it feels like alignment. When meetings have no conflict, it feels like cohesion. When direct reports do not push back, it feels like trust. In reality, teams that never disagree are often teams that do not feel safe enough to be honest. The smooth surface hides a psychological safety problem that will eventually surface as turnover, disengagement, or an unexpected failure.

## How Leadership Coach Agent Helps

This agent coaches through structured frameworks (GROW, Situational Leadership, Psychological Safety), but it leads with questions, not answers. It assesses leadership style, identifies situational mismatches, diagnoses team dynamics, and helps leaders adapt their approach to different people and situations.

The core principle: leaders create the weather. If your team is silent, the question is not "why won't they speak up?" but "what have I done that makes speaking up feel unsafe?" The agent holds up a mirror before offering a map.

## A Story

James, a newly promoted engineering director, runs smooth meetings where everyone agrees with his proposals. His skip-level asks a pointed question: "Either you are always right, or your team is not telling you the truth. Which one do you think it is?"

The Leadership Coach diagnoses the pattern: James grew up technically, where being right was the job. As a leader, his precision signals "I have already decided," making pushback feel like insubordination rather than collaboration. His team is not aligned, they are compliant.

The fix is surprisingly small. James changes one question: instead of "Any concerns?" (which implies there should not be any), he asks "What worries you about this plan?" (which implies concerns are expected). He also starts sharing his own doubts first, modeling that uncertainty is acceptable.

Six weeks later, his skip-level notes: "Your team argues more now. That is progress."

## Step-by-Step

1. **Describe your challenge**: What leadership situation are you facing? People dynamics, team issues, or personal development
2. **Share the context**: Team composition, organizational culture, your tenure in the role
3. **Identify who is involved**: Specific people and their roles in the dynamic
4. **State your goal**: What does success look like?
5. **Work through GROW**: The agent guides you through Goal, Reality, Options, Will
6. **Get actionable plans**: Specific behaviors to change, conversations to have, experiments to run

## Skills at a Glance

| Skill | What It Does | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Coaching Conversation | GROW-based 1:1 coaching session | Any leadership challenge requiring structured reflection |
| Leadership Style Check | Diagnoses your style, team fit, and needed adaptations | When something is not working and you cannot see why |
| Team Health Check | Assesses trust, psychological safety, and team dynamics | When engagement is low, turnover is high, or conflict is absent |
| Growth Plan | Focused leadership development plan with specific milestones | When you want structured development, not just advice |

## Choosing a Personality

| Personality | Best For | Energy |
|------------|---------|--------|
| Believer | Leaders who need encouragement and confidence alongside challenge | Warm, optimistic, uses analogies and stories |
| Executive Coach | Senior leaders who want strategic, results-focused coaching | Direct, efficient, cuts to the leadership behavior that matters |
| Mentor | Leaders who learn through shared experience and collaborative reflection | Experienced, collaborative, story-driven wisdom |

## When to Use Another Agent

Leadership coaching addresses people leadership, not every organizational challenge. Escalate when:

- **Cross-cultural team dynamics**: hand off to [Culture Agent](culture-agent-guide.md) when cultural dimensions are driving the friction
- **Structured decision analysis**: hand off to [Six Hats Agent](six-hats-agent-guide.md) when the challenge is a decision, not a people problem
- **Root cause investigation**: hand off to [Why Agent](why-agent-guide.md) when team symptoms point to deeper systemic issues
- **Stakeholder politics**: hand off to [Corporate Navigator Agent](corporate-navigator-agent-guide.md) when the challenge is organizational power, not team dynamics
- **Mental health or therapy**: redirect to professional support when leadership coaching is insufficient
