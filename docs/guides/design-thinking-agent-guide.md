# Design Thinking Agent

*Guides human-centered problem-solving through design thinking, helping teams move from empathy to testable solutions using structured divergent and convergent thinking.*

## The Problem

Your team is about to spend six months rebuilding a tool that users complain about. Everyone agrees the interface is the problem. The backlog is full of UI improvements. But no one has asked users what they actually need, and the assumption that "better interface = better adoption" is about to consume a quarter of engineering capacity.

## The Trap

Teams often jump from complaint to solution without understanding the real problem. "Users hate the tool" becomes "redesign the interface" without ever investigating why users hate it. The most expensive mistake in product development is building the right solution to the wrong problem. UI redesigns feel productive because they produce visible change, but visible change is not the same as meaningful change.

## How Design Thinking Agent Helps

This agent walks teams through the five stages of design thinking: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test. The non-linear process forces you to understand users before proposing solutions, frame the problem before generating ideas, and test assumptions before investing resources.

The critical shift happens in the empathy stage. When you map what users actually experience (their goals, frustrations, workarounds, and unspoken needs), the problem definition often changes completely. What looked like a usability problem might be a motivation problem, a trust problem, or a workflow problem that no interface change can fix.

## A Story

An operations head is frustrated that only 41% of consultants submit time entries on time despite a functional tracking tool. The obvious fix: redesign the interface to make it faster and more intuitive. Engineering scopes a six-month rebuild.

Before committing, the Design Thinking Agent runs an empathy sprint. The findings change everything: consultants do not struggle with the interface. They struggle with motivation. Time tracking gives them nothing back, so they batch entries on Fridays from memory. The data is inaccurate because the process feels pointless, not because the UI is confusing.

The reframed problem: "How might we make daily time entry personally valuable to consultants?" The solution is not a prettier form. It is a daily personal dashboard showing utilization trends, project distribution, and billing milestones. Three months later, submission compliance reaches 87%. The six-month UI rebuild never happens.

## Step-by-Step

1. **Define your starting point**: Are you just starting, have initial insights, or need to test something?
2. **Describe the problem context**: Who is affected, what is happening, what constraints exist
3. **Identify stakeholders**: Who are the users and decision-makers?
4. **Work through the stages**: The agent guides empathy mapping, problem definition, ideation, prototyping, and testing as needed
5. **Get structured outputs**: Empathy maps, insight statements, HMW questions, prioritized ideas, prototype plans, or test learnings depending on your stage

## Skills at a Glance

| Skill | What It Does | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Empathy Sprint | From stakeholder understanding to problem definition | When you think you know the problem but have not validated with users |
| Ideation Session | From defined problem to prioritized ideas | When you have a clear problem statement and need creative solutions |
| Prototype & Test | From idea to validated learning | When you have ideas and need to test assumptions cheaply |
| Full Design Sprint | End-to-end design thinking cycle | When starting from scratch and need the complete process |

## Choosing a Personality

| Personality | Best For | Energy |
|------------|---------|--------|
| Facilitator | Team workshops, inclusive brainstorming, group alignment | Energizing, inclusive, keeps momentum |
| Coach | Teaching the methodology, building team capability | Patient, educational, explains the why |
| Strategist | Quick strategic application when time is limited | Direct, action-oriented, cuts to essentials |

## When to Use Another Agent

Design thinking is a discovery and ideation framework, not an analytical or research tool. Escalate when:

- **Deep source analysis needed**: hand off to [Research Agent](research-agent-guide.md) for multi-source research and fact-checking
- **Multi-perspective evaluation**: hand off to [Six Hats Agent](six-hats-agent-guide.md) when you need structured parallel thinking across perspectives
- **Root cause investigation**: hand off to [Why Agent](why-agent-guide.md) when the problem needs depth before breadth
- **Stakeholder politics blocking progress**: hand off to [Corporate Navigator Agent](corporate-navigator-agent-guide.md)

The Design Thinking Agent does not replace actual user research, provide UX/UI design deliverables, make final design decisions, or manage project timelines.
