# Corporate Navigator Agent

*Makes invisible workplace power structures visible and turns political awareness into actionable, ethical strategy.*

## The Problem

Your proposal got killed in committee for the second time. The feedback is vague: "not the right time," "needs more alignment." You know the idea is sound. You suspect the real reason has nothing to do with the idea itself, but you cannot see the actual dynamics well enough to respond.

## The Trap

Most people react to political obstacles by pushing harder on merit. They refine the deck, add more data, request another meeting. But if the objection is not about merit, more evidence will not help. The real dynamics, who holds informal power, whose territory you are crossing, what resource burden your proposal creates, stay invisible while you optimize the wrong variable.

## How Corporate Navigator Agent Helps

This agent treats office politics as a system to understand, not a game to win. It maps stakeholder interests, influence, and relationships. It names political dynamics explicitly: gatekeeping, coalition-building, sponsorship, territory protection. Most importantly, it helps you understand why decisions really get made, which is rarely the stated reason.

The shift from "my idea is good, why won't they approve it?" to "what is actually happening in this decision system?" changes everything. Once you see the map, you can navigate it ethically.

## A Story

Elena is a product director whose strategic proposal gets killed twice by a VP of Risk citing "regulatory complexity." She is preparing a third attempt with more compliance data when she pauses to map the landscape.

The agent's influence-planning skill reveals the real dynamic: the VP's objection is not philosophical (he does not disagree with the strategy), it is operational (his already-stretched team would bear the compliance burden). The words say "regulatory risk," the behavior says "don't add to my workload."

Elena changes approach entirely. Instead of proving regulatory viability, she restructures the proposal to include a dedicated compliance resource. She repositions the VP from obstacle to co-owner. The third presentation gets approved in 20 minutes. Same proposal, different political navigation.

## Step-by-Step

1. **Describe the organization context**: Industry, company stage, culture. The political landscape varies dramatically between a startup and a Fortune 500
2. **Define your role and situation**: What are you trying to accomplish? Where are you stuck?
3. **Name key stakeholders**: Who matters for this decision? Include formal and informal influencers
4. **Receive the landscape analysis**: The agent maps interests, alliances, and power dynamics
5. **Get recommendations**: Top 3 strategies ranked by impact, with risk assessment and confidence level

## Skills at a Glance

| Skill | What It Does | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Map Stakeholders | Identifies key players, their interests, power, and relationships | Starting any political navigation, new role, or new initiative |
| Analyze Dynamics | Maps the political landscape: alliances, informal networks, power flows | When decisions are being made and you do not understand why |
| Plan Influence | Develops ethical strategies for gaining support and building coalitions | When you need specific tactics to move an initiative forward |
| Career Coach | Structured development using GROW and strengths-based frameworks | When your career is stalled and you suspect political factors |
| Navigate Situation | Tactical advice for specific scenarios with 24-72 hour action window | When you need to respond to a political situation right now |

## Choosing a Personality

| Personality | Best For | Energy |
|------------|---------|--------|
| Executive | Strategic, high-level analysis of power structures and organizational dynamics | Measured, perceptive, systems-level thinking |
| Coach | Supportive development of political awareness and self-advocacy skills | Patient, encouraging, builds confidence |
| Tactical | Direct, practical advice for immediate situations with clear action steps | Sharp, action-oriented, 24-72 hour focus |

## When to Use Another Agent

The Corporate Navigator maps political terrain, it does not solve every workplace problem. Escalate when:

- **Cross-cultural dynamics** are the primary friction: hand off to [Culture Agent](culture-agent-guide.md)
- **Root cause investigation** needed beyond political surface: hand off to [Why Agent](why-agent-guide.md)
- **Difficult interpersonal conversation** required: hand off to [Difficult Conversations Agent](difficult-conversations-agent-guide.md)
- **Harassment, discrimination, or ethical violations**: these require HR or legal channels, not political strategy
- **Mental health concerns**: redirect to professional support
